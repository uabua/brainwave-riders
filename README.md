# Brainwave Riders

**SSVEP (Steady-State Visual Evoked Potential) BCI data analysis project**
*developed during the [BR41N.IO Spring School 2026](https://www.br41n.io/) hackathon.*

---

## What is this?

When you stare at a flickering light, your brain produces electrical oscillations at the same frequency - this is called a **Steady-State Visual Evoked Potential (SSVEP)**. A Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) can detect which frequency you're looking at and use it as a command, enabling communication without movement.

This project analyzes SSVEP data from 2 subjects across 2 sessions each, recorded with 8 posterior EEG electrodes (PO7, PO3, POz, PO4, PO8, O1, Oz, O2) while they focused on LEDs flickering at **9, 10, 12, and 15 Hz**.

Explore raw EEG signals, frequency-domain features, topographic maps, and classification results for 2 subjects across 4 recording sessions - all in your browser.
